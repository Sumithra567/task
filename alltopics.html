About Dr. Kalam

Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam, known as the "Missile Man of India," was a visionary scientist and the 11th President of India. Born in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, he rose from humble beginnings to become a key figure in India's space and defense programs. His leadership in projects like the Pokhran-II nuclear tests and his inspirational role in education made him a national icon.

Timeline of Life

1931

Born in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu.

1969

Joined ISRO and led India’s first satellite launch vehicle (SLV-III).

1998

Played key role in India’s Pokhran-II nuclear tests.

2002-2007

Served as the 11th President of India.

2015

Passed away while delivering a lecture at IIM Shillong, doing what he loved most—teaching and inspiring.
